Hi Daniel,

The AFP already contains such a formalisation, very similar to your approach:
https://www.isa-afp.org/browser_info/current/AFP/Ordinary_Differential_Equations/Bounded_Linear_Operator.html <https://www.isa-afp.org/browser_info/current/AFP/Ordinary_Differential_Equations/Bounded_Linear_Operator.html>

setup_lifting is documented in the Isabelle/Isar Reference Manual (11.9.2 Lifting Package).
https://isabelle.in.tum.de/dist/Isabelle2021/doc/isar-ref.pdf <https://isabelle.in.tum.de/dist/Isabelle2021/doc/isar-ref.pdf>

In your example, "setup_lifting type_definition_blinalg" sets up the Lifting Package such that you can define constants involving the type тАЬтАШa blinalgтАЭ in terms of constants involving the concrete type тАЬ(тАШa, тАШa) blinfunтАЭ.

For the instantiation of тАЬnorm_blinalg", you have to provide a definition for the type that you want to instantiate ("тАШa blinalgтАЭ), i.e., the following should work (with "setup_lifting type_definition_blinalgтАЭ after your typedef):

lift_definition norm_blinalg :: "'a blinalg тЗТ real" is norm .

Hope this helps,
Fabian
