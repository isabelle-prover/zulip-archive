Dear all,

The deadline of the International Workshop on Logical and Semantic
Frameworks with Applications (LSFA 2024) has been extended. The new
deadline is 14 June 2024, with abstracts to be submitted at 7 June.

Find below the updated call for papers.

The 19th International Workshop on Logical and Semantic Frameworks with
Applications (LSFA 2024) will be held on September 18-20 2024 in
Goiânia, Brazil.
For more information, see https://sites.google.com/ufg.br/lsfa2024

IMPORTANT DATES
Abstract registration deadline June 7 2024
Submission deadline June 14, 2024
Notification date               August 7, 2024
Workshop                        September 18-20, 2024
Final version due               November 1, 2024

TOPICS

Logical and semantic frameworks are formal languages used to represent
logics, languages and systems. These frameworks provide foundations for
the formal specification of systems and computational languages,
supporting tool development and reasoning.

  • Automated deduction
  • Applications of logical and/or semantic frameworks
  • Computational and logical properties of semantic frameworks
  • Formal semantics of languages and systems
  • Implementation of logical and/or semantic frameworks
  • Lambda and combinatory calculi
  • Logical aspects of computational complexity
  • Logical frameworks
  • Process calculi
  • Proof theory
  • Semantic frameworks
  • Specification languages and meta-languages
  • Type theory

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

All papers must be unpublished and not simultaneously submitted to
another journal or conference. The following paper categories are welcome:

  • Full papers with a maximum of 16 pages (not including references or
    appendixes)

  • Short papers with a maximum of 6 pages (not including references or
    appendixes)

The papers should be prepared in latex using EPTCS style, and submitted
as a PDF file in https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=lsfa2024 . All
contributions should be written in English.

PUBLICATION

The pre-proceedings, containing the reviewed extended abstracts, will be
handed-out at event registration. After the meeting the authors will be
invited to submit full versions of their works for the post-proceedings
publication in EPTCS (TBC). At least one of the authors should register
for the conference. Presentations should be in English.

According to the quality of submissions, the chairs will promote further
publication of journal revised versions of the papers. Previous LSFA
Special Issues have been published in journals such as The Logical
Journal of the IGPL, Theoretical Computer Science and Mathematical
Structures in Computer Sciences (see the LSFA page
https://lsfa-workshop.github.io/).
