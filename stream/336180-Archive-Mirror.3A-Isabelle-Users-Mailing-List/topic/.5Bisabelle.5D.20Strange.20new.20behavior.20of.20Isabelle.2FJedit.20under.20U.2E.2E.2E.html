The issue is that when Isabelle/Jedit exits, sometimes there are many, many shell prompts
that are printed, seemingly as if the shell was suddenly notified about the exit of a whole
bunch of child processes all at once. For example, below is shown what I just saw after exiting
a session that has gone on for a half-day or so.

In this case, I exited the session because it had become unresponsive for many minutes,
as it tends to do with unpleasant frequency. You can see all the % prompts from the shell
upon exit. You can also see what I consider to be an abnormal number of prompts printed
when I restarted the session.

There is nothing relevant in the syslog. When it has occurred to me to do so, I have looked for
dead process build-up and have found nothing. The contents of my "etc/settings" file:

ISABELLE_PLATFORM="${ISABELLE_PLATFORM64:-$ISABELLE_PLATFORM}"
ML_PLATFORM="${ISABELLE_PLATFORM}"
ML_HOME="${POLYML_HOME}/x86_64-linux"
ML_OPTIONS="--maxheap 12000"

This system has 24GB of RAM. I last rebooted 20 days ago. I don't recall for sure whether this
new behavior was associated with the reboot or whether it occurred before. I believe it occurred
before and I had thought that it might be the result of some library upgrade or something,
and was surprised to see that it persisted after the reboot.

I don't really know what to make of this. It started a month or so ago. Anybody have a clue?
